# Stick-Figure Cast

## Role

The recurring visual element is not a branded mascot — it is the **generic stick-figure actor**: the person living through the experience. By default every panel contains at least one actor, and the actor performs the core action of that step.

If you could delete the figure and the panel still reads exactly the same, the figure is decoration — rework it so the figure *is* the action.

## The actors

- **Visitor / learner** (primary): the person using the experience. Points, places a sample, holds a tablet, watches the screen, reaches toward AR, reacts. Drives most panels.
- **Facilitator / guide** (optional): a second figure who hands off, gestures toward the exhibit, or assists. Use only when the step genuinely needs two people.

## Form

- Simple round head (open circle), thin straight-line torso and limbs, small line hands/feet.
- Even, clean line weight — black/dark ink outline, no fill.
- No face details, or at most two dot eyes; no hair, no clothing detail.
- Proportions human-ish and relaxed, not chibi, not heroic.
- A figure may hold one simple prop (tablet, sample, pointer) — keep props line-art too.

## Emotion through posture

Show feeling with the body, not facial expression:

- Curiosity / engagement: leaning in, one arm pointing or reaching.
- Delight / success: both arms raised.
- Concern / disruption: hand to head, stepping back, slight slump.
- Calm takeaway: standing, holding a tablet, a small speech bubble nearby.

## Props & companions

- Tablet / phone, pointer, coral or specimen sample, a small line-art device.
- Speech / thought bubbles (rounded) for "chat", AI, or a takeaway — a small heart, node-graph, or audio waves inside.
- Small line-art sea life (fish, jellyfish, turtle) as the *content* the actor interacts with — not as mascots.

## Forbidden

- No cute mascot styling, big sparkly eyes, or expressive cartoon face.
- No fully rendered/detailed human, no realistic anatomy, no shaded body.
- No colored body fill — figures stay line-art.
- No figure that just stands in a corner watching while the "real" content is elsewhere.
- Don't let the figure crowd out the step's structure; it acts within the layout, it doesn't dominate it.

## Test

Remove the figure mentally. If the step's meaning survives unchanged, the figure was decorative — rewrite the prompt so the figure carries the action (placing, pointing, reaching, reacting).
