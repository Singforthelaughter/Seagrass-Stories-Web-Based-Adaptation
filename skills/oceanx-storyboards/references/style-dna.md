# Style DNA

## One line

Clean, friendly, line-art process storyboard — white background, navy structure, teal flow, light-blue digital washes, minimalist stick-figure actors. A calm museum-exhibit explainer, slightly techy, never cute, never photoreal.

It should look like a thoughtful product/exhibit designer sketched the journey on a whiteboard, then tidied it into a numbered strip.

## Must

- Wide horizontal layout (banner / multi-panel strip). 16:9 or wider is fine; the examples are wide ribbons of 3–5 panels.
- Clean **white background**. No paper texture, no gradient backdrop, no noise, no vignette.
- **Numbered steps**: a filled dark-navy circle with a white numeral at the top-left of each panel, followed by a bold navy step title.
- **Teal block arrows** between panels, pointing in the direction of flow (usually left → right).
- **Minimalist black stick-figure actors** performing each step's core action (see `stick-figure-cast.md`).
- Light-blue / cyan washes to mark screens, water, AR projection, and digital fields.
- Generous whitespace — panels breathe, subjects don't fill the frame.
- Thin, even line work for icons, props, and figures. Hand-clean, not wobbly, not heavy.

## Palette

Keep it restrained. Most of the image is white + black line art + navy + teal. Warm color is an accent, not a theme.

- **Navy (ink ~ #0B2545 / #10243F)**: step circles, step titles, panel frames/labels, key structure, the darkest line work.
- **Teal / cyan (~ #16B3AC / #1FA8A0)**: flow arrows, AR glow, active-state highlights, the "this is the through-line" color.
- **Light blue (~ #BFE6F2 → #E6F4FA gradient)**: screens, water, AR projection volumes, "digital" surfaces. Soft, low-saturation fills.
- **Warm accent (yellow / orange, rare)**: a focal creature (a fish or two), a single highlight. Use on at most 1–2 small elements per image.
- **Digital-disruption navy pixels**: a scatter of small dark-navy squares to show glitch, data, or ecological disruption.
- **White**: background and negative space.

Stick figures are black/dark-ink outline. Don't color-fill them.

## Absolutely never

- No photorealism, no 3D render, no rendered lighting.
- No detailed faces, hair, or clothing on the figures — they are stick figures.
- No flat full-color "corporate stock vector" illustration style.
- No cute mascot / kawaii cartoon characters.
- No literal pixel-perfect UI mockup (this storyboards the experience, it is not the screen).
- No drop shadows, bevels, glows-as-decoration, heavy outlines, or textured backgrounds.
- No dense paragraphs of text inside the image.
- No rainbow palette or large saturated color fills.
- No missing or out-of-order step numbers.

## Aesthetic direction

Friendly, clear, modern, a little techy, calm. Like a well-designed science-museum panel or a clean explainer in a product deck.

Not childish, not corporate-glossy, not busy, not photoreal.
