# Cast & Settings

## The cast

Authentic, varied, candid people — not stock-photo models. Mix ages and ethnicities naturally. Show real engagement through posture and gaze.

- **Kids (≈7–12)**: curious, animated, pointing and touching. Great for kiosk and touch-table shots.
- **Teens / students (≈13–18)**: focused, collaborative, often leading or explaining. Good at big screens and data tools.
- **Families**: a parent with one or two children, leaning in together at a touch table. Warm, intergenerational.
- **OceanX facilitator / volunteer**: a teen or adult in an **OceanX-branded navy tee or polo**, pointing at the screen and guiding the group. Use when the shot needs a guide or to signal the brand.
- **Home user**: one person (a student or adult) at a desk or on a couch, sometimes a second person nearby. Calm, absorbed.

Casting rules:
- Keep faces natural; no uncanny features, no identical twins of the same model.
- Engagement reads through the body and eyes, not a posed grin at the camera.
- One clear primary actor per shot; others support.

## The two settings

### Exhibition / museum

- Dim, atmospheric gallery: deep blue walls, spotlights, glass cases, a backlit ocean mural, blurred visitors behind.
- The screen/exhibit is the main light source on faces; cool ambient with warm accents.
- Devices: wall-mounted touchscreen kiosk (sometimes a webcam on top), a round/curved touch table, a large curved panoramic screen, a mounted companion tablet.
- Often OceanX branding on a facilitator's shirt or a screen corner.

### Home

- Cozy, daylit room: a desk by a window, a monitor or laptop, or a couch with a tablet.
- Everyday life around it: mug of tea, a plant, books, a backpack, soft furnishings.
- Calmer and warmer than the exhibition; one absorbed user, maybe a sibling or parent nearby.
- The web experience runs in a normal browser/app window on a consumer device.

## Matching cast to setting

- Exhibition kiosk / touch table → kids or a family, optionally a facilitator.
- Big curved data screen → students + an OceanX facilitator explaining.
- Home desktop / tablet → an individual student or adult, occasionally a second person.

Pick the combination that makes the on-screen content believable and the interaction natural.
