# QA Checklist

## Must pass

- Photorealistic — reads as a real photograph, not illustration, render, or CGI.
- The on-screen content reflects the **real project** (correct title, recognizable UI/visual, plausible controls).
- The setting is clearly exhibition (dim blue, screen-lit, blurred gallery) or home (warm daylight, cozy props).
- Authentic, candid people; one clear primary actor performing a believable interaction.
- Both the person and the screen are readable.
- Natural lighting appropriate to the setting; shallow-to-medium depth of field.
- Varied, natural casting; no uncanny faces.
- OceanX branding present only where it makes sense (facilitator shirt, screen corner) and not garbled.

## Failure signals

Regenerate or edit if you see:

- Illustration / cartoon / vector / 3D-render / videogame-cutscene look.
- Plastic or uncanny skin, distorted hands, melted faces.
- Stock-photo posing — people grinning at the camera, stiff arrangement.
- A generic dashboard that doesn't match the project, or a blank/placeholder screen.
- On-screen title or labels garbled beyond a believable level.
- Flat, evenly-lit, characterless lighting; or fake flares / heavy HDR / oversharpening.
- A sci-fi floating hologram instead of a real screen/AR touch glow.
- Added watermarks, caption bars, or logo lockups the model invented.
- Wrong setting cues (a museum that looks like an office, a "home" with no warmth).

## How to iterate

- Screen content wrong/generic: restate the on-screen-content block with the exact title, main visual, and one or two real controls.
- Garbled screen text: prefer a local edit to fix the title; keep on-image text short.
- Too stocky/posed: ask for a candid moment, gaze on the screen, no eye contact with camera.
- Looks illustrated/CGI: re-emphasize "photorealistic editorial photography, natural light, real skin texture, shallow depth of field; not a render."
- Flat lighting: specify the key light (screen-lit faces in the dim gallery; window daylight at home).
- Brand off: place OceanX only on a facilitator's shirt or a screen corner; remove invented logos.

## Delivery test

A good shot looks like a real, warm photo a museum or education brand would publish — a believable person genuinely engaged, with the actual experience clearly running on the screen. If it looks rendered, posed, or the screen could be any random app, it fails.
