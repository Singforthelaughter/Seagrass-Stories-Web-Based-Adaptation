# Photo Style

## One line

Authentic, documentary/editorial photography of people genuinely using an OceanX web experience — natural light, shallow depth of field, candid engagement, the project's real UI glowing on the screen. Believable, warm, and a little cinematic. Never stocky, never illustrated, never a 3D render.

## Must

- Photorealistic photography. Real-looking people, real materials, real light.
- A real device with the **project's actual content on screen** (see `prompt-template.md`'s on-screen block).
- Natural, candid interaction: pointing, touching a slider, leaning in, talking, reacting. Caught-in-the-moment, not lined up for the camera.
- Believable lighting for the setting (see below).
- Shallow-to-medium depth of field; subject sharp, background softly blurred (other visitors, exhibits, a window).
- A clear focal subject (the person interacting) and a clear secondary read (what's on the screen).
- Natural skin tones, varied and authentic casting.

## Lighting & color by setting

- **Exhibition / museum**: dim, moody ambient with cool blue wash; screens and exhibits are the key light on faces; warm spill from a window or spotlight for contrast. Deep blues and teals, pools of light, gentle bloom from screens. Cinematic but real.
- **Home**: soft daylight from a window, warm and cozy; a desk with a monitor or a couch with a tablet; everyday props (mug, plant, books, backpack). Brighter, calmer, lower contrast than the exhibition.

## Lens & framing

- 35mm–85mm full-frame look. Slight wide for environmental kiosk/table shots; tighter for over-the-shoulder screen shots.
- Eye-level or slightly low, near the subjects' height (kid-height for kid shots).
- Over-the-shoulder framing works well to show both the person and the screen.
- Rule-of-thirds, some negative space; don't center everything.

## Absolutely never

- No illustration, cartoon, vector, or hand-drawn look (that's the storyboards skill).
- No 3D render / CGI / videogame-cutscene look; no plastic skin, no uncanny faces.
- No flat, evenly-lit corporate stock-photo vibe; no forced grins at the camera.
- No fantasy holograms floating unrealistically (a subtle AR/touch glow is fine; a sci-fi hologram is not).
- No heavy HDR, oversharpening, or fake lens flares.
- No watermarks, no caption bars, no logo lockups added by the model.
- No misrepresenting the project: the screen should reflect the real experience, not a random generic dashboard.

## Aesthetic direction

Warm, human, curious, real. The kind of photo a good museum or an education brand would use on a landing page or in a grant deck — people clearly *into* the experience, with the product doing real work on screen.
